# Client Brief Template

Blank brief template to send the client **before** launching `/site`. If filled in ahead of the kickoff, the Brief phase of `/site` becomes a validation pass instead of a discovery interview.

Send as a Notion doc, Google Doc, or email — whichever suits the client. Keep the copy jargon-free; this document is for non-technical people.

---

## How to Use (Internal Note — remove before sending)

- Duplicate this file and rename to `brief-<client-name>.md`.
- Remove this "How to Use" section and the internal notes in parentheses.
- Share the rest with the client.
- When returned, load into `/site` as the Brief input. The assistant will flag gaps and propose the 3 most important follow-up questions rather than re-running the full interview.

---

# Brief — [Site Name]

**Client** — _______________________
**Date** — _______________________
**Contact(s)** — _______________________

Thanks for taking the time to fill this in. Reply with your own words — precision isn't needed at this stage. We'll sharpen everything together during the technical kickoff.

---

## 1. Your Company

**Q1.** Who are you, in one plain sentence? (Imagine explaining your business to someone you just met.)

**Q2.** What's your sector, your team size, and how long have you been running?

**Q3.** Three direct competitors (URLs please).

**Q4.** What genuinely sets you apart — beyond marketing language? (What do your best customers say about you?)

---

## 2. Your Audience & Goals

**Q5.** Who visits this site? Describe one typical visitor: age range, profession, how familiar they are with your field, which device they likely use.

**Q6.** What is the **one** action you most want visitors to take? (Contact, request a quote, sign up, buy, download, book a call…)

**Q7.** How will you know the site is a success three months after launch? Give one concrete number if possible (conversions per week, demos booked, leads, signups).

---

## 3. Content & Tone

**Q8.** Who writes the site copy — you, your team, an agency, or do you need us to write it?

**Q9.** Describe your tone of voice in three adjectives. (Examples: direct, warm, expert / confident, playful, informed / quiet, precise, technical.)

**Q10.** Which languages does the site need to support at launch? At one year?

**Q11.** Roughly how many pages do you expect? (One long single page / 3-5 pages / 10+ pages / also needs a blog or news section?)

---

## 4. Visual Mood

**Q12.** Give us three to eight sites that make you think *"yes, that direction"* (URLs). They don't have to be in your industry — it's about the look and feel.

**Q13.** Give us three to eight sites you want to **avoid** at all costs (URLs), even if they're popular.

**Q14.** Overall visual atmosphere — pick one or describe: light / dark / colorful / monochrome / editorial / minimalist / other?

**Q15.** Typography preference — modern and neutral / classic and elegant serif / kinetic and expressive / a mix / no opinion?

---

## 5. Motion & Interactivity

**Q16.** Ambition for animation on a scale of 1 to 5.
- **1** — clean, simple, no animation distractions
- **2** — subtle fade-ins and hover effects
- **3** — smooth scroll, light parallax, polished but discreet
- **4** — strong motion signature, scroll-driven transitions
- **5** — immersive experience, Awwwards-level (+budget impact)

**Q17.** Smooth scroll (inertia-based, cinema-like scrolling) — yes / no / let us decide?

**Q18.** Any specific effects you like? Parallax, custom cursor, scroll-driven storytelling, video background, animated text…

**Q19.** Do you want 3D or WebGL elements? (Be aware: this typically adds ~30% to budget and timeline.)

---

## 6. Your Content Assets

**Q20.** **Photos** — do you have your own, do you want us to source from a library, do you want a professional shoot, or don't know yet?

**Q21.** **Videos** — how many, how long each, and do you want video in the hero section?

**Q22.** **Logo and brand** — Figma file available, just final files, or does it need to be designed/refreshed as part of this project?

**Q23.** **Illustrations, icons, pictograms** — do you have an existing set, should we create one, or are standard icons fine?

---

## 7. Technical

**Q24.** Do you need a CMS? (A CMS lets you edit text and images yourself without a developer. Good if content changes often; unnecessary for a mostly static site.)

**Q25.** What integrations must the site support? (Examples: Google Analytics, contact form emailing you, newsletter signup, Calendly booking, live chat, e-commerce, Stripe payments, CRM like HubSpot.)

**Q26.** Do you have mobile performance constraints? (Slow sites get penalized in Google search. Any specific target — fast on 4G, first-paint under 1.5s, etc.?)

---

## 8. SEO & Distribution

**Q27.** Three to ten keywords you'd like to rank on in Google search. (Don't overthink — terms your ideal customer would type.)

**Q28.** Is your business a specific type requiring structured data? (Local business with address, product catalog, article/news, event, recipe…)

**Q29.** **Open Graph images** — the preview card shown when your link is shared on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, or messaging apps. Do you have designed images, or should we create them?

---

## 9. Execution

**Q30.** Target delivery date (launch or first working version).

**Q31.** Intermediate review — do you want us to pause for your review at specific milestones (e.g. after design, after dev)? How many review rounds?

**Q32.** **Domain and hosting** — domain already registered (which one?), need us to buy one, or any hosting constraint on your side?

---
